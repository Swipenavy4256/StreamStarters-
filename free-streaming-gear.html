Gear Guide

Best Free & Cheap Gear
for New Streamers

You don't need to spend hundreds of dollars to start streaming. Here's exactly what you need — most of it free.

One of the biggest myths about streaming is that you need expensive gear to get started. Wrong! Millions of successful streamers started with just a phone or a basic laptop. Here's what actually matters — and what you can get for free.

Free Streaming Software

📺
100% Free

OBS Studio

The most popular streaming software in the world. Free, powerful, and works on Windows, Mac, and Linux. Great for customizing your stream layout.

⚡
Free (with paid options)

Streamlabs

Built on OBS but easier for beginners. Comes with built-in alerts, overlays, and widgets. Perfect if you want your stream to look polished right away.

📱
Free

YouTube / Twitch Mobile Apps

Stream directly from your phone with zero extra software. Tap "Go Live" in the app and you're done. Perfect for starting out before you get a PC setup.

💡 Recommendation

If you're on a computer, start with OBS Studio. If you're on a phone or tablet, just use the YouTube or Twitch app directly. Both are completely free and work great for beginners.

Audio: Your Most Important Tool

Here's a secret most beginners don't know: audio matters more than video. Viewers will watch a blurry stream with clear audio, but they'll leave a crystal-clear stream with bad audio. Get this right first.

🎧
Free (if you have them)

Phone Earbuds / Headset

The microphone on your phone earbuds is surprisingly decent for streaming. Plug them in and use them as your mic — works much better than your built-in laptop mic.

🎤
~$20–$40

Budget USB Microphone

When you're ready to upgrade, a simple USB mic like the Fifine K669 (~$25) makes a huge difference. Plug and play, no setup needed. Your viewers will notice immediately.

Video: Start with What You Have

💻
Free (already have it)

Built-in Laptop Webcam

Your laptop's built-in camera is perfectly fine for starting out. Many viewers prefer streamers who show their face — it feels more personal and real.

📷
Free (already have it)

Your Smartphone Camera

Your phone's camera is better than most webcams! Use an app like DroidCam (free) to use your phone as a webcam for your computer. Works great.

💡 Lighting Tip

Good lighting makes any camera look 10x better. Face a window or put a lamp in front of you (not behind). Natural light from a window is completely free and works amazingly well.

Free Extras That Make a Difference

  • Stream overlays: Sites like Nerd or Die and Streamlabs offer free overlays and alerts
  • Background music: Use royalty-free music from Pretzel.rocks or StreamBeats — both free for streamers
  • Stream panels: Download free Twitch panels from sites like OWN3D.tv to make your channel look professional
  • Canva: Free tool to design your logo, banner, and thumbnail graphics
  • Discord: Create a free server for your viewers to hang out in between streams
