Twitch Guide

How to Start Streaming
on Twitch

Twitch is the #1 live streaming platform for gamers. Here's how to set up your channel and go live for the first time.

By the StreamStarters Team · Last updated July 22, 2026

Twitch is all about live streaming — unlike YouTube, people come to Twitch specifically to watch streams happening right now. It has a huge gaming community, but you can stream art, music, cooking, or anything you enjoy. Best of all, it's completely free to use.

Is Twitch Right for You?

✅ Twitch is great if you...

  • Love gaming and want a gaming community
  • Want to interact live with viewers in real time
  • Plan to stream regularly
  • Want to eventually earn from subscriptions

⚠️ Consider YouTube if you...

  • Also want your stream saved as a video
  • Want a broader audience beyond gaming
  • Already have a YouTube following
  • Plan to post highlights and clips
⚠️ Age Requirement

You must be at least 13 years old to create a Twitch account. If you're under 13, ask a parent to create the account and stream together with you supervising.

Step-by-Step: Set Up Your Twitch Stream

Step 1

Create Your Twitch Account

Go to twitch.tv and click "Sign Up." Pick a username — this is your streamer name, so choose something cool! Add a profile picture and write a short bio about what you stream.

Step 2

Download OBS Studio

OBS Studio (obsproject.com) is the most popular free streaming software. Download it for free on Windows, Mac, or Linux. Run the auto-setup wizard — it handles most settings automatically.

Step 3

Connect OBS to Twitch

In OBS, go to Settings → Stream → choose "Twitch" as the service. Click "Connect Account" and log in to Twitch. OBS will automatically get your stream key. That's it — you're connected!

Step 4

Add Your Game or Screen

In OBS, click the "+" under "Sources" and choose "Game Capture" (to capture a game) or "Display Capture" (to show your whole screen). Preview the stream to make sure everything looks right.

Step 5

Set Your Stream Title & Category

Go to twitch.tv and click "Channel" → "Stream Manager" → "Edit Stream Info." Add a fun title and pick the correct game category. A good title + category helps people discover your stream.

Step 6

Hit "Start Streaming" 🚀

In OBS, click "Start Streaming." Check Twitch to confirm you're live. Now start talking, playing, and having fun. Say hi to viewers when they show up!

Twitch Tips for Beginners

  • Stream consistently — even once or twice a week builds a loyal audience
  • Pick a category carefully — some games are very crowded, making it hard to be discovered
  • Try "Just Chatting," art, or smaller game categories where new streamers get noticed more easily
  • Network with other small streamers — support each other's streams
  • Enable Twitch's AutoMod feature to filter bad words in chat
  • Clip your best moments and share them on TikTok or YouTube Shorts
💡 Pro Tip

The hardest part about Twitch is the discovery problem — it's hard to be found with zero viewers. The fix? Don't rely only on Twitch. Post clips, engage in Discord servers, and post your stream schedule on Reddit or Twitter so people can find you.

Twitch Affiliate & Earning Money

Once you reach 50 followers, stream for 8 hours across 7 different days, and average 3 viewers — you can apply for Twitch Affiliate. As an Affiliate, viewers can subscribe to your channel (starting at $4.99/month) and you keep half. You can also earn from Bits (virtual tips) and ad revenue.

💡 For Young Streamers

If you're under 18, you'll need a parent or guardian to handle any earnings or payment setup. Always include a parent in any decisions about monetization.
