YouTube Guide

How to Start Streaming
on YouTube

A simple, step-by-step guide for beginners. No experience needed — you can go live today!

YouTube is one of the best places to start streaming because almost everyone already has an account, and it's completely free. Whether you want to stream gaming, art, music, or just hang out and chat — YouTube Live has you covered.

What You'll Need to Start

  • A Google/YouTube account (free — ask a parent if you're under 13)
  • A computer, phone, or tablet
  • A stable internet connection
  • Something to stream — a game, a hobby, whatever you enjoy!
  • Optional: Free streaming software like OBS (for computers)
💡 Good to Know

YouTube requires your account to be verified before you can go live on desktop. This just means confirming your phone number — ask a parent to help. On mobile, you can go live right away if your channel is at least 50 subscribers.

Step-by-Step: Go Live on YouTube

Step 1

Set Up Your YouTube Channel

Go to YouTube.com and sign in. Click your profile icon → "Your Channel" → customize your channel name and add a profile picture. Make it something fun and memorable!

Step 2

Choose How You'll Stream

On mobile (phone/tablet): Download the YouTube app. Tap the "+" icon at the bottom, then "Go Live." Easy!

On computer: Download free software called OBS Studio (obsproject.com). It's free, beginner-friendly, and used by millions of streamers.

Step 3

Set Up OBS (Computer Only)

Open OBS and run the auto-setup wizard. Add a "Scene" and a "Source" (like your game or screen). Then go to Settings → Stream → pick YouTube and paste your Stream Key (found in YouTube Studio → Go Live).

Step 4

Create Your Stream Title & Description

In YouTube Studio, click "Go Live." Write a fun title like "Playing Minecraft for the first time!" and add a short description. A good title helps people find your stream.

Step 5

Go Live! 🚀

Click "Go Live" in OBS or the YouTube app. You're streaming! Say hi, be yourself, and have fun. Your first stream probably won't be perfect — and that's totally okay.

Tips for a Great YouTube Stream

  • Talk out loud even when nothing is happening — silence feels awkward
  • Respond to every chat message when you're small — viewers love when you notice them
  • Set a schedule and stick to it (even just once a week)
  • Use a good title with keywords people would search for
  • Stream what you genuinely enjoy — it shows!
  • Keep your streams to 1–2 hours to start — don't burn out
⚠️ Safety Reminder

Never share your real name, school, address, or phone number on stream. If a viewer says something that makes you uncomfortable, don't engage — just ban or block them. Check out our full safety guide for more tips.

Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting a stream without testing your mic/camera first
  • Quitting after your first few streams with low viewers
  • Not saying anything for long periods of time
  • Using copyrighted music (YouTube will mute or strike your stream)
  • Forgetting to end the stream properly

Ready to Go Live? 🎉

You've got everything you need. The hardest part is clicking that button for the first time.
