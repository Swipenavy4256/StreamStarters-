How to Stream on Twitch
With Just Your Phone

No gaming PC. No capture card. No problem. Everything you need to go live on Twitch is already in your pocket.

By the StreamStarters Team · Last updated July 22, 2026

One of the biggest myths in streaming is that you need an expensive PC setup to get started. The truth? Thousands of streamers go live every day using nothing but a phone. If you've been waiting to start streaming until you can afford a computer, stop waiting — here's exactly how to do it today.

Two Ways to Stream From Your Phone

There are two totally different kinds of mobile streaming, and which app you need depends on which one you want to do.

1

IRL Streaming (Official Twitch App)

If you want to stream yourself — talking to chat, doing art, playing guitar, cooking, whatever — the official Twitch app does this natively. Open the app, tap your profile picture, tap "Go Live," give your stream a title, pick a category, and you're broadcasting from your phone camera. That's genuinely it.

2

Mobile Game Streaming (Streamlabs Mobile)

The official Twitch app can't capture your phone screen — so to stream mobile games like Fortnite, Roblox, or Clash Royale, you need a third-party app. Streamlabs Mobile is the most popular free option. It captures your screen, overlays your camera and chat, and broadcasts straight to your Twitch channel.

Step-by-Step: Your First Mobile Stream

  • Download the Twitch app and log into your account
  • For screen/game streaming, download Streamlabs Mobile and connect your Twitch login
  • Connect to Wi-Fi (mobile data works, but Wi-Fi is way more stable)
  • Set a stream title that says what you're actually doing
  • Pick the right category — this is how people find you
  • Prop your phone somewhere stable or use a cheap tripod
  • Tap Go Live and talk like someone's watching (even when nobody is yet)
💡 Do This Before Going Live

Turn on Do Not Disturb mode. Nothing kills a stream faster than your notifications popping up on screen — and it also protects your privacy by keeping texts and DMs off your broadcast.

What About Stream Quality?

Phone streams won't match a PC setup, and that's fine. Aim for 720p at 30fps — it looks clean, and it's much less likely to buffer than forcing 1080p over Wi-Fi. In Streamlabs Mobile you can set this under video settings. You need about 3–5 Mbps of upload speed; you can check yours for free at speedtest.net. If your stream stutters, drop the resolution before you blame the app.

⚠️ Watch Your Battery and Heat

Streaming is heavy work for a phone. Keep it plugged in, take the case off if it gets hot, and close every other app before you go live. An overheating phone will lower its own performance mid-stream — which your viewers will notice.

Making Your Phone Stream Look Better for Cheap

You don't need to buy anything to start, but if you have a little budget, three things upgrade a mobile stream more than anything else: a phone tripod (a few dollars online), a clip-on ring light for even lighting, and wired earbuds with a mic so your voice sounds close and clear instead of echoey. Frequently Asked Questions

Can you stream on Twitch with just a phone?

Yes. The official Twitch app streams from your camera for IRL content, and Streamlabs Mobile captures your screen for mobile gaming. No PC or capture card needed.

Can you make money streaming from a phone?

Yes — mobile streamers can reach Twitch Affiliate just like PC streamers. The requirements are identical. See our Twitch Affiliate guide for exactly what you need.

How much internet speed do I need?

At least 3–5 Mbps upload for stable 720p streaming. Use Wi-Fi whenever possible — mobile data speeds jump around too much for a smooth stream.
