Twitch Affiliate Requirements
Explained in Plain English

Your first step toward making money on Twitch. Here's exactly what you need, how long it takes, and what to expect when you get there.

By the StreamStarters Team · Last updated July 22, 2026

Twitch Affiliate is the first monetization milestone every streamer aims for — it unlocks subscriptions, bits, and your first real income from streaming. The requirements are public, but Twitch explains them in a confusing way. Here they all are, in plain English, plus the honest answers about timelines and money that most guides skip.

The 4 Requirements (All in the Last 30 Days)

You need to hit all four of these at the same time, measured over a rolling 30-day window:

500

500 Minutes Broadcast

That's about 8.5 hours of streaming total in 30 days. If you stream 2 hours twice a week, you'll pass this easily. This is the easiest requirement.

7

7 Unique Broadcast Days

You need to stream on 7 different days within the 30-day window. Two streams on the same day count as one day. Consistency matters more than marathon sessions here.

3

3 Average Concurrent Viewers

This is the hard one. You need an average of 3 people watching at the same time across all your streams. Not 3 total viewers — 3 at once, averaged over every minute you're live.

50

50 Followers

Fifty people need to hit the follow button on your channel. With consistent streaming and the right game categories, this usually comes naturally alongside the viewer requirement.

⚠️ The Trap Most New Streamers Fall Into

Streaming to zero viewers for 10 hours actually hurts your average viewer count. If nobody's watching, a shorter stream with 2–3 friends dropping in beats a marathon stream to an empty room. The average is calculated per minute you're live.

How Long Does It Actually Take?

Honest answer: 1–3 months for most people who stream consistently 3+ times a week. Some hit it in two weeks (usually people whose friends show up), and some take six months. The 3 average viewers requirement is almost always the bottleneck — which is why picking the right game category matters so much. Streaming Fortnite as an unknown streamer means competing with 10,000 other channels. Streaming a smaller game with an active community means viewers actually scroll far enough to find you. How Much Do Affiliates Actually Earn?

Let's be real: your first months as an Affiliate will earn you roughly $10–50/month. Subscriptions cost viewers $5.99, and you keep 50% of that. Bits add a little more. It's not a paycheck — it's proof of concept. The streamers earning real money got there by treating Affiliate as mile one, not the finish line.

💡 Set Up Before You Qualify

Once you hit the requirements, Twitch sends an invite to your dashboard. You'll need to fill out tax forms (yes, even minors need a parent's help with this) and set up payouts. Don't panic — it's a one-time process, and Twitch walks you through it.

Your Affiliate Game Plan

  • Stream at least 3 days a week, 2+ hours each — that covers minutes and days automatically
  • Pick game categories where you can be on the first page (under ~20 live channels)
  • Tell friends when you go live — 2 regulars nearly carries the viewer requirement
  • Talk constantly, even to zero viewers — lurkers follow streamers who seem alive
  • Check your progress in Creator Dashboard → Insights → Achievements

Frequently Asked Questions

Do the requirements reset?

No — it's a rolling 30-day window. Your stats from 31+ days ago stop counting, but you don't start over. Keep streaming and the numbers build.

Can you lose Affiliate status?

Twitch can remove Affiliates for long inactivity (12+ months) or rule violations, but you won't lose it for a slow month. Once you're in, you're in.

Is there an age requirement?

You must be at least 13 to stream on Twitch, and under-18 streamers need a parent or guardian to handle the Affiliate tax and payment paperwork.
