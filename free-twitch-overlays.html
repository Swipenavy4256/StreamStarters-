Best Free Twitch Overlays
(And How to Add Them)

Make your stream look professional without spending a dollar or knowing anything about design.

By the StreamStarters Team · Last updated July 22, 2026

An overlay is the graphic frame around your stream — the webcam border, the "Starting Soon" screen, the little banners showing your latest follower. A clean overlay instantly makes a small stream feel like a real channel. And despite what overlay shops want you to believe, you can get genuinely good ones for free.

The Best Free Overlay Sources

1

Nerd or Die (Free Section)

Professional-quality animated overlays, alerts, and stream packages. Their free section is better than most paid stuff elsewhere. You can pay $0 at checkout — sliding to free is allowed and encouraged for new streamers.

2

Streamlabs Built-In Themes

If you use Streamlabs Desktop instead of plain OBS, it has a built-in theme library with hundreds of free full overlay packages that install in one click. Easiest option by far for total beginners — no files to manage at all.

3

OWN3D Free Overlays

Mostly a paid shop, but their free section rotates solid packages — especially clean, minimal designs that don't scream "free template." Requires an email signup.

4

Canva (Make Your Own)

Search "Twitch overlay" in Canva's free templates and customize colors and text to match your channel. Export as PNG with a transparent background. This is how you get an overlay nobody else has — for free.

⚠️ Avoid Sketchy Download Sites

Only download overlays from known sites like the ones above. Random "free overlay pack" downloads from forums or YouTube descriptions can contain malware. If a site asks you to disable your antivirus or run an .exe file for a "graphics pack," close the tab.

How to Add an Overlay to OBS (Step by Step)

  • Download your overlay — you'll usually get PNG image files
  • In OBS, click the + button in the Sources panel
  • Choose "Image" and select your overlay PNG
  • Drag the corners to fit your screen — hold Alt to crop edges
  • In the Sources list, drag the overlay ABOVE your game capture so it sits on top
  • For animated overlays or alerts, choose "Browser" as the source instead and paste the widget URL the overlay site gives you
💡 The Layer Rule

OBS sources stack like paper — whatever is higher in the Sources list appears on top. Standard order from top to bottom: alerts, overlay frame, webcam, game capture. If your overlay is invisible, it's almost always just under your game layer.

What Actually Matters (And What Doesn't)

Here's the secret: viewers don't follow streams because of overlays. A clean webcam border and readable alerts are all you need to look put-together. Streamers with the fanciest $200 overlay packages and no personality still sit at zero viewers. Get something simple, then spend your energy on the things that actually grow a channel — being consistent, picking the right categories, and talking to your chat. Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an overlay to start streaming?

No. Plenty of successful streamers use nothing but a webcam and gameplay. Overlays are polish, not a requirement — don't let overlay shopping delay your first stream.

Do animated overlays cause lag?

One or two browser-source overlays are fine on most computers. If you're already fighting performance issues, check our guide to fixing OBS lag before adding more moving parts.

Can I use the same overlay on YouTube?

Yes — overlays live in OBS, not on Twitch. The same setup broadcasts to any platform, including YouTube. See our YouTube streaming guide.
